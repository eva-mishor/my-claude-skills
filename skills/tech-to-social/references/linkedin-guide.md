# LinkedIn Technical Post Guide

## Platform Constraints

- **Post body limit**: 3,000 characters (including spaces)
- **First 2-3 lines visible** before "...see more" fold — this is the hook
- **Line breaks**: Double line breaks create visual spacing; use generously
- **No markdown rendering**: Bold/italic don't render. Use CAPS, brackets, or unicode sparingly for emphasis
- **Hashtags**: 3-5 per post, placed at the end. Mix broad (#SoftwareEngineering) with niche (#CompressionAlgorithms)
- **Links in body**: LinkedIn suppresses reach for posts with external links. Put links in first comment instead
- **Images/carousels**: Posts with visuals get 2-3x engagement. Suggest image ideas when relevant

## Post Structure Templates

### The Story Arc (for challenges/debugging/decisions)
```
[Hook: surprising outcome or relatable problem]

[Context: 2-3 sentences on what you were working on]

[The challenge: what went wrong or what decision you faced]

[The insight: what you discovered or decided]

[The result: concrete outcome with numbers if possible]

[Takeaway: 1-2 actionable lessons]

[Hashtags]
```

### The Listicle (for tips/patterns/comparisons)
```
[Hook: bold claim or question]

[Brief context: 1-2 sentences]

Here's what I found:

1. [Point] - [brief explanation]
2. [Point] - [brief explanation]
3. [Point] - [brief explanation]

[Wrap-up insight]

[Hashtags]
```

### The Show-and-Tell (for demos/results/achievements)
```
[Hook: impressive result with specific number]

[What we built: 2-3 sentences]

Key results:
- [Metric 1]
- [Metric 2]
- [Metric 3]

[What made this possible / what we learned]

[Hashtags]
```

### The Series Intro
```
[Hook: scope of the series topic]

Over the next [N] posts, I'll break down:

[Post 1 preview]
[Post 2 preview]
[Post 3 preview]

[Why this matters / who benefits]

Follow along if you [target audience description].

[Hashtags]
```

## Tone Calibration

### DO
- Write like you're explaining to a smart colleague over coffee
- Use "I" and "we" — personal experience resonates
- Include specific numbers, metrics, timelines
- Show vulnerability: mistakes, wrong assumptions, pivots
- End with something actionable the reader can use

### DON'T
- Use corporate buzzwords ("leverage", "synergy", "paradigm shift")
- Be condescending or over-explain basics
- Make it sound like a press release
- Use AI-sounding phrases ("In the realm of", "It's worth noting that", "Let's dive in")
- Hedge everything — take a position

## Series Planning

When planning a multi-post series from a large technical effort:

1. **Identify the narrative arc**: What's the overall story? (problem -> exploration -> solution -> results)
2. **Find natural breakpoints**: Each post should be self-contained but build on the series
3. **Vary the format**: Mix story posts, listicles, and show-and-tell across the series
4. **Front-load the hook**: The first post should make people want to follow the series
5. **Suggest posting cadence**: 2-3 posts/week for a series, spaced to maintain momentum
